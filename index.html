Semester Reflection

These labs have allowed me to construct interactive web pages using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. I have been able to implement my work, such as Tableau Visualizations and Folium maps, within a cohesive website designed for an easy follow along for viewers.

The AI experimentation and evaluation lab allowed me to critically assess how AI functions. I was able to experiment with errors produced by AI, as well as how to develop these errors into functional outcomes. An emphasis that was important with AI was to identify which different AI tools could handle certain inputs versus which are better suited for other tasks. This lab developed my sense of how to use AI as a tool and recognize when AI might fail. Critical AI skills such as these are especially important to understand the depth behind AI so that usage can be optimized.

For the Visualization Critique lab, I read excerpts from Edward Tufte's Beautiful Evidence and identified the importance of identifying deceitful visualizations and why they cause harm. For the visualization, “Homicides are Falling around the world,” I identified two instances of “Cherry-Picking” and the “Rage to Conclude.” This critique allowed me to fully investigate the credibility of a visualization and identify what additional information is needed.

The Tableau Visualization lab developed my skills with designing visualization using Tableau Public. I was able to learn how to properly clean and prepare the dataset. I went into depth exploring different features that Tableau has and experiment with their outcomes. I also explored design choices that would properly show patterns and clearly portray the data. My developed abilities in Tableau though this lab have allowed a more crucial understanding on how to create visualizations.

For the Vibe Coding lab, I was able to explore ways that AI can help produce code for a specific outcome. I learnt ways to present very specific directions to AI and saw what worked and what didn’t work. This could be seen as detailing in the prompt which type of regression I wanted to use and how I wanted the visualization made. I also learnt how to deal with errors and modify AI generated code.

Lastly, for the Hometown Map lab, I learnt how to design a map, use folium and python to create and implement the custom map. This lab taught me the most because I had never created a personalized map, and I was able to see real-world applications from these designs. This lab shows my ability to develop a creative project from scratch and adhere elements to a specific theme.
